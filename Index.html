The Ancient Wonder of Africa

Great Zimbabwe is a medieval city in the south-eastern hills of Zimbabwe near Lake Mutirikwe and the town of Masvingo. It was the capital of the Kingdom of Zimbabwe during the country's Late Iron Age.

Constructed between the 11th and 15th centuries, the city spans 7.22 square kilometers and could have housed up to 18,000 people at its peak. The stone constructions, built without mortar, stand as a testament to the architectural ingenuity of the Shona civilization.

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Great Zimbabwe remains one of Africa's most significant archaeological sites and a source of national pride.

Key Attractions

The Great Enclosure

The largest ancient structure in sub-Saharan Africa, featuring an impressive 11-meter high wall extending approximately 250 meters.

The Hill Complex

The oldest part of Great Zimbabwe, believed to be the spiritual and political center of the kingdom.

The Valley Complex

A series of ruins suggesting this was the main residential area for the city's elite.

Zimbabwe Birds

Iconic soapstone carvings that served as emblems of royal authority, now a national symbol.
